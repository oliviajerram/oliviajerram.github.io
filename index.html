I recently graduated from UC Berkeley with a degree in astrophysics and a passion for science communication. During my time at the university, I worked for four years at The Daily Californian, Berkeley's student-run newspaper of record, including a term as the publication's managing editor. About At the Roots

What is At the Roots?

As a queer woman with a STEM degree, and as someone with a strong journalistic background who has noticed the lack of diversity in science reporting, I want to use my voice to uplift others’ from communities that are often underrepresented. At the Roots is a series of features on scientists whose stories have historically been excluded from the media for reasons including but not limited to their gender identity, sexual orientation, race and ethnicity, disabilities and religious views.

Why start now?

Knowing that it is the responsibility of individual journalists to work to deconstruct the biases and reporting gaps of long-standing publications, I want to do my part to change the way the press reports on science and its scientists. At the root of the matter, there is a need to present the narratives of those who, growing up, were not able to see themselves represented in mass media, as I was, and that is what this project aims to do.

Who should participate?

At the Roots will focus on the graduate students in the Bay Area, and across the country, who are responsible for the bulk of scientific research at universities, and telling their stories. Because science should not aim to be removed from culture and emotion, I hope to unite the personal experiences of these researchers with the ground-breaking work they are often performing.
