Hello,
I'm Olivia Jerram,

a science journalist & educator

I recently graduated from UC Berkeley with a degree in astrophysics and a passion for science communication. During my time at the university, I worked for four years at The Daily Californian, Berkeley's student-run newspaper of record, including a term as the publication's managing editor. Upon leaving the Daily Cal, I began writing for the Rothfels Lab on campus, communicating the team's research on plant phylogenetics to the public. Now, I hope to begin a project of my own: spotlighting Bay Area graduate students in STEM. Read on for more about Project _______.

About Project _____

What is Project _____?

As a queer woman with a STEM degree, and as someone with a strong journalistic background who has noticed the lack of diversity in science reporting, I want to use my voice to uplift othersâ€™ from communities that are often underrepresented. Project ____ is a series of features on scientists whose stories have historically been excluded from the media for reasons including but not limited to their gender identity, sexual orientation, race and ethnicity, disabilities and religious views.

Why start now?

Knowing that it is the responsibility of individual journalists to work to deconstruct the biases and reporting gaps of long-standing publications, I want to do my part to present the narratives of those who, growing up, were not able to see themselves represented in mass media, as I was, and to change the way the press reports on science and its scientists.

Who should participate?

I harbor a deep love of the Bay Area and the Cal campus, and for this reason, I would like to focus on local scientists, specifically the graduate students who are responsible for the bulk of scientific research at universities, and telling their stories. Because science should not aim to be devoid of culture and emotion, I hope to unite the personal experiences of these researchers with the ground-breaking work they are often performing.
